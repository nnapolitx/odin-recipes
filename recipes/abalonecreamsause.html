Abalone Cream Sauce

Back to Homepage

Description

This delicious cream sauce fusions the flavors of traditional Chilean cusine with Italian and French influences. The best part about this dish is it can serve many people with few ingredients. It can also be served with fresh or sauteed greens. While its sophisticated appearance will impress your diners, this dish will impress you with its simplicity and few ingredients.This recipe will serve up to four adults

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 200g/7 oz. of abalone or about six medium-sized abalone
  • 200g dried spaghetti (1 package)
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, Divided!
  • 5 large garlic cloves
  • 1/3 cup of white wine (the cheaper the better)
  • 1 cup of thickened cream
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup of chopped parsley
  • salt and pepper or red pepper flake to taste

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook, stirring occasionally, until just tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, place a large pan over medium heat. Add the butter to the pan. Once the butter is melted, add garlic and cook until fragrant (about 30 seconds to 1 minute). Do not brown.
  3. Pour in wine and bring to a simmer for 2 minutes or until wine reduces by half.
  4. Add cream and allow to simmer until slightly thickened.
  5. Remove pan from the heat. Stir in lemon juice and add the spaghetti and abalone to the pan to warm through slightly. Garnish with parsley.
  6. Serve with pan-friend asparagus or any other fresh or sauteed green (optional)
